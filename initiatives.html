Empowering Women through Strategic Initiatives

Initiatives web page on Safeguard features significant safety programmes focusing on women, such as the Analysis Wing, Anti-Human Trafficking, and Cyber Security Modules. Every section contains an overview, key objectives, and contact information, channeling visitors towards the right support services. This web page thus empowers women, with direct accessibility to specific resources-a safe haven towards a safer future.

ANALYSIS WING

Overview

The analysis module of Women Safety Wing helps the different modules of the government by the study of the data related to women and children cases, which reduces crimes against them. It helps the Special Task Force locate an accused in POCSO cases also helping track victims and suspects in kidnapping, cybercrime, as well as human trafficking investigations.

Key Objectives

  • SHE Software functions in active mode to receive complaints through She Teams WhatsApp, CCTNS, Darpan Application and Child Track Portal. Missing children's data will be updated while running analysis reports on cases in the C-DAT system.
  • Manage the social presence of the Women Safety Wing with the PR team.
  • Organize the awareness program along with volunteers and other relevant officers through office memos via e-mails.

Anti-Human Trafficking Unit

Overview

The Women Safety Wing under the Anti-Human Trafficking Module of the organization formulates an inclusive national policy on protection, prevention, rescue, and rehabilitation against trafficking. Acting as the nodal agency, it exercises oversight over AHTUs in all States/UTs. Besides these endeavours, the organization has coordinated with stake holders working in the same direction with all its might to curb human trafficking.

Key Objectives

The Anti-Human Trafficking Module equips the victims with comprehensive support to protect them from further danger, timely rescue, and rehabilitation services like counseling and vocational training. It offers awareness programs that sensitize the public on their vulnerability to trafficking. Strengthening of Anti-Human Trafficking Units occurs through coordination with law enforcement bodies and NGOs. The unit monitors and analyzes the data of trends in such activities to provide legal support to victims at litigation.

Cyber Module

Overview

There is the Cyber Module that deals with cybercrimes against women and children. The module tracks complaints, aids officers across the country, and accumulates information on statuses and actions taken in response to these complaints. Through this, the module enhances decisions and maximizes standard operating procedures-an effective factor in the quality of investigations.

Key Objectives

The Cyber Module Introduces the analysis of trends and methodologies based on reports and data by Cyber Cell units. It manages the petitions reported to the Women Safety Wing pertaining to content removal, FIR registrations, etc. In addition, it up-skills officers on investigative techniques and coordinates with SHE Teams and the Bharosa modules in conducting awareness camps and outreach programs.

Domestic Violence Module

Overview

The Safeguard Domestic Violence Module aims to address issues on domestic violence and dowry harassment. It keeps track of cases while creating a platform for raising more awareness while working as a coordinator between diverse groups interested in the safety of women and children, thereby ensuring swift responses to affected parties.

Key Objectives

Domestic violence is a form of abuse from a biological relative, mainly affecting women at the hands of male family members. This ranges from physical violence to mental, oral, sexual, and economic harming that makes someone incapable of having their rights assert against perpetrators. Some of the other vital acts include the Dowry Prohibition Act of 1861, prohibiting dowry in general, and sections 498A and 304B of the Indian Penal Code, which were enacted in 1983 and 1986, respectively. Later, PWDVA was enacted in 2005 which added a new definition to domestic violence by defining its mode of action.

NRI CELL

Overview

Safeguard's NRI Cell deals with matters where the spouses or relatives are an NRI and is in the list of accused. This initiative will be used to help distraught women abandoned by their NRI partners by collaborating with embassies and the National Commission for Women. It offers legal guidance and support to domestic violence and maintenance cases.

Key Objectives

NRI Cell in helping deserted women by NRI spouse with legal consultancy and support. It coordinates with embassies and organizations and facilitates the platforms to share experiences. Through legal consultancy, the Cell raises awareness about women's rights in NRI marriages and provides for connected legal support.

Missing Persons Monitoring Cell

Overview

Missing Persons Monitoring Cell Effective use of state-of-the-art technology to deal with cases of missing persons with dignity An integrated cell was formed in collaboration with UNICEF, WCD department, and NIC to trace out missing people and prevent them from becoming involved with any social evil so that they may return to their families or are re-settled.

Women Help Desks

Overview

As part of the Safeguard initiative, the Central Government has put in place Women Help Desks, which are the first point of contact for distressed women, acting as a one-stop support center. These Help Desks provide police station emergency assistance all over the country-personal contact and 24×7 helpline service. The teams are specifically trained to empathize with victims and are sensitized to various women safety issues, whereby women are treated with care who seek help in such circumstances and receive the necessary support and redressal.

Key Objectives

It aims to make police stations friendlier and more accessible to work units, which may help decrease secondary victimization of abused women. It provides comprehensive emergency service with medical care needs of the victim and counseling, shelter, mental health services, and skills enhancement. The program also highlights rescue, prevention, and protection activities for women in such cases, alongside coordinating activities with other police stations and district AHTUs.

Safety Guidelines for Women Tourist in India

Overview

Safeguard is an initiative towards the safety of foreign women in India. This application allows easy registration of complaints while allowing access to other travel services like travel applications, hotlines for tourists, and government actions-Tourist Police and the service related to women's safety. It also provides verified transportation, providing a good atmosphere for foreign women traveling in India. The platform enables quick help and assistance during the journey towards a safer travel.

Key Objectives

One of the key objectives of the Safeguard initiative for foreign women's safety in India will include facilitating an easy method for complaint registration through the app and allowing access to all travel services, with a dedicated hotline for emergencies at the tourist helpline. It helps in coordination with every kind of government-related organizations, like Tourist Police and also the women safety services. It helps them ensure verified transportations and even raises more awareness about the rights of foreign women traveling and their safety as well. It also offers medical assistance and legal support information, further educating service providers of culture.
