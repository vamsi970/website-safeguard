Government Schemes for Women’s Education and Empowerment

This website provides access to numerous schemes initiated by the Government of India for women and girls, mainly in relation to education and skill development. The aim of these schemes is to bridge gender gap, as well as to provide equal opportunities to women and girls through financial assistance, educational incentives, and technical training. These schemes thus improve the social and economic status of women and make them participate actively in nation-building while removing problems like gender bias and economic inequality.

Overview

BBBP is launched in the year 2015 that addresses the issue of gender discrimination and works for the survival, protection, and education of a girl child. The main objectives of BBBP are to shift the declining child sex ratio, further spread awareness in respect of gender equality, and ensure girls' education and their empowerment.

Key Objectives

The prime objectives of the Beti Bachao Beti Padhao (BBBP) scheme are as follows:

  • Social practices that favor daughters over sons may lessen or avoid gender-biased sex-selective elimination.
  • Survive and Safety Protection of Girls: Promote Health, Safety, and Equal Opportunities.

Benefits

The BBBP scheme offers its advantages through awareness campaigns, education incentives, and better infrastructural facilities at schools for girls to promote access to education, reduce dropout rates, and bring about a better gender balance, empowering them to seek education and development in a friendly environment.

UJJAWALA SCHEME

Overview

The UJJAWALA Scheme was launched in the year 2007 to combat and prevent trafficking of women and children for commercial sexual exploitation. It offers them rehabilitation, rescue, and reintegration support and ensures respect for their rights so that they can be reintegrated into society.

Key Objectives

The main objectives of the UJJAWALA Scheme are to prevent trafficking of women and children for commercial sexual exploitation, rescue and rehabilitation services for victims, awareness generation about human trafficking, and comprehensive reintegration into society.

Benefits

The UJJAWALA Scheme has the rescue and rehabilitation facilities offered to the victims of human trafficking, including shelter and health care, skill development and vocational training in skills, and providing legal aid to ensure justice is done. It also envisions activities in raising awareness to educate communities about how not to get trafficked.

Overview

This is the Support to Training and Employment Programme for Women which aims at women's employability and entrepreneurship through skilling in areas such as traditional crafts, hospitality, and health. The program empowers marginalized women by giving them skills, placing them in a job, and funding them to set up their own enterprises.

Key Objectives

The STEP aims to enhance women's employability and encourage entrepreneurship through trainings in skills development in different sectors. The goal is to enable marginalized women empowerment, provide them with employment opportunities, facilitate self-employment with financial access, and ultimately strive for gender equality and economic independence.

Benefits

Support to Training and Employment Programme for Women- STEP provides skills building training aiming at promoting employability; assists in placement so that trained women may be brought together with employers; provides financial support to entrepreneurial ventures, and thus enables marginalized women, gender equality, and economic independence.

SUKANYA SAMRIDDHI YOJANA

Overview

Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana is a scheme for saving the financial future of girl children that is state-backed to ensure availability at attractive interest rates and tax benefits along with the facility to draw the amount for expenditure on schooling and dowry. Thus, it promotes long-term savings.

Key Objectives

The main objectives of the Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana are:

  • Savings for the girl child, for the future financial security in respect of education as well as marriage.
  • Supporting long-term planning for families helps reduce financial pressure during major life events, like higher education and marriage of the girl child.

Benefits

Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY) offers high interest rates that are tax-free, has deposits and tax exemptions under Section 80C, provides long-term financial security for the girl child, helps with education and marriage expenses, and promotes savings across all income groups with government-backed financial protection.

NATIONAL SECHEME OF INCENTIVES TO GIRLS FOR SECONDARY EDUCATION (NSIGSE)

NATIONAL SECHEME OF INCENTIVES TO GIRLS FOR SECONDARY EDUCATION (NSIGSE)

If you want to know more, please click below button to redirect to the official site.

Click Here

Overview

The National Scheme of Incentives to Girls for Secondary Education initiates the continuation of education among disadvantaged SC/ST community girls and others. It will offer a cash incentive of Rs 3,000 accessible when the girl attains 18 years and remains unmarried, to address the issue of such dropout rates and promote education.

Key Objectives

The key objectives of NSIGSE include the following:

  • Promote secondary education among girls and also the SC/ST and socio-economically weaker sections.
  • Promote gender equality by empowering and educating marginalized girls while reducing dropout rates through monetary incentives after completing Class VIII.

Benefits

The incentive scheme has only a one-time lump sum of INR 3,000 under the National Scheme of Incentives to Girls for Secondary Education (NSIGSE), which motivates girls from all sections of society to continue their education further with low dropout rates and, thereby promote empowerment of girl's equality through secondary education support.

UDAAN SCHEME

Overview

The Udaan Scheme offers opportunities to economically disadvantaged girls for higher education, especially in science and engineering streams. It provides free online resources, mentorship, and exam preparation services for entry exams like JEE among others with a view for greater participation of girls in STEM fields.

Key Objectives

The key targets of the Udaan Scheme are:

  • Promotion of STEM education among girls, especially from economically backward groups.
  • Academic support through free online resources and mentoring for competitive exams like JEE and also encourage girls in STEM to boost female participation in engineering..

Benefits

This Scheme provides free online resources, mentoring, and exam preparations for the JEE to girls with disadvantageous backgrounds. It will help more females increase participation rates in STEM fields because the scheme encourages girls to study further in science and engineering.

Overview

Kasturba Gandhi Balika Vidyalaya : This is an innovative scheme where the girl child in rural areas belonging to SC, ST, OBC and minorities is offered free residential education. The scheme aims at promoting access to quality education by girls and girls' participation in class 6-12.

Key Objectives

The Kasturba Gandhi Balika Vidyalaya programme aims to fill rural education disparities by keeping girls from the marginalized section through residential schooling. Improvement in the learning outcomes of students and focus on holistic development, including life skills and vocational training for future opportunities.

Benefits

The Kasturba Gandhi Balika Vidyalaya scheme is providing residential education free of cost to girls belonging to poorer sections by offering boarding, food, and schooling up to the 12th grade. Kasturba Vidyalaya scheme helps fulfill the objectives of more access to quality education and lesser drop-outs with life skills, vocational training, and better academic growth.

PRADHAN MANTRI MATRU VANDANA YOJANA

Overview

Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana it awards Rs 5,000 to pregnant and lactating women by three installments. This scheme targets mother's nutrition and proper antenatal care during pregnancy and childbirth for the better health of both mother and the infant.

Key Objectives

The Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana focuses on providing financial support to pregnant and nursing women with the aim of better nutrition and health along with early registration of pregnancy, adequate antenatal and postnatal care at the right time, and developing awareness on maternal health along with exclusive breastfeeding for six months.

Benefits

The cash benefit of ₹5,000 is provided to the expectant and lactating mothers under the **Pradhan Mantri Matru Vandana Yojana, apart from facilitating better maternal care, reducing costs associated with childbirth, and encouraging healthy practices such as antenatal checkup and exclusive breastfeeding among mothers and their infants.
